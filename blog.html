The NCLEX is No Joke

The National Council Licensure Examination is a high-stakes, adaptive exam that every nursing graduate must pass to become a licensed RN. The passing standard is rigorous — you're not just answering questions, you're demonstrating clinical judgment at a safe practice level.

And then in 2023, NCSBN made it harder. The Next Generation NCLEX introduced six new question formats that test clinical judgment more deeply: Matrix Grids, Bow-tie items, Enhanced Hotspots, Drop-down Cloze, Ordered Response, and Extended Drag & Drop. The exam doesn't just want you to know facts — it wants you to think like a nurse at the bedside.

💡 Pro Study Tip
When practicing, don't just memorize answers — understand the clinical reasoning. Ask yourself: why is this option wrong? What would happen to the patient if I chose it? That's what the NCLEX is actually testing.

Clinical Domains Covered

  • Management of Care — delegation, prioritization, ethical/legal
  • Safety & Infection Control — standard precautions, fall prevention, error reporting
  • Health Promotion & Maintenance — screenings, developmental stages, lifestyle
  • Psychosocial Integrity — mental health, therapeutic communication, coping
  • Basic Care & Comfort — ADLs, mobility, comfort measures
  • Pharmacology & Parenteral Therapies — drug classes, IV therapy, calculations
  • Reduction of Risk Potential — lab values, diagnostic tests, complications
  • Physiological Adaptation — acute care, emergencies, hemodynamics

Track Your Progress Over Time

Every session is automatically saved to your browser's local storage — so your progress persists even after you close the tab. When you're ready to study on a different device or just want a backup, hit Save to download a JSON file with your complete history.

💾 How Progress Tracking Works
Your progress file stores: total questions answered, overall accuracy %, per-domain scores across every session, per-question history (seen, correct, last seen), and a full session log. Load it back anytime to pick up exactly where you left off — sessions accumulate and merge, never overwrite.

The progress dashboard shows your weakest domains front and center so you know exactly where to focus. Session history is sortable by date, mode, score, and time. It's the study loop that paid tools charge for — here it's just built in.

Completely Free. Always.

No account. No subscription. No paywalls. Just open the link and start practicing. The entire simulator runs in a single HTML file — you can even download it and run it offline.

The AI clinical insights require an internet connection (they're powered by Anthropic's Claude API), but if that's unavailable, the simulator falls back to hand-written tips and rationales for every question.
